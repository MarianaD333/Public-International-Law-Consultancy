ICJ Advisory Opinion on Climate Obligations: A Landmark for Global Justice and Litigation

The International Court of Justice held hearings in December 2024 to address countries' legal obligations on climate change, following a 2023 request by the United Nations General Assembly. Whilst the resulting advisory opinion is non-binding, it is anticipated to shape global climate litigation and significantly influence international legal and policy frameworks.

November 2024

UK Transfers Sovereignty of Chagos Islands to Mauritius Following ICJ Ruling

In November 2024, the United Kingdom transferred sovereignty of the Chagos Islands to Mauritius, following a 2019 ruling by the International Court of Justice, which declared the UK's control unlawful under international law. The decision upholds the principle of self-determination and seeks to rectify the colonial injustices faced by the Chagossian people.
